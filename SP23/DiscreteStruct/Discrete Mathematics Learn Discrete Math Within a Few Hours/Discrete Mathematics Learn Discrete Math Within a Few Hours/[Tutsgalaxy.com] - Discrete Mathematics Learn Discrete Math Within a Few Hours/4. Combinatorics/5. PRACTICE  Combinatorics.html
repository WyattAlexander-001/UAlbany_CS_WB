Here are a couple of examples about combinatorics, I recommend you solve these, and if you get stuck, as allways feel free to post a question. 

  1. In how many ways you can choose 5 of 32 playing cards not considering their order?
  2. You have group of 5 people from which you are choosing 3 to represent your firm at a conference, In how many ways you can choose those people whithout considering their order?
  3. How many words can you make from letters 'a', 'b', 'c', 'd', When you want the words to have length of "n"?































Spoiler Alert








Solutions:

1. 32 choose 5 =  32! / (27! * 5!) = 201376

2. 5 choose 3 = 5! /  (2! * 3!) = 10

3. 4 to the power of n
